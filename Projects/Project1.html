Web Site Critiques

Questions

  • What was your first impression of this site?
  • How does this site organize the flow of information?
  • Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  • Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  • How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  • Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  • Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  • Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  • How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  • What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Trader Joe's (https://www.traderjoes.com)

I found my first impression of this website to be satisfied. I enjoy the color scheme, easy to read fonts, navigation and most importantly the layout of the website. You can tell they used cascading style sheets and most likley Javascript with how clean the design of the webpage is.

Trader Joe's organizes it's information from top to bottom, it's a couple pages long as well. Along with that, the website has its own side navigation menu, as well as a search bar. I also noticed a picture is provided, followed by a short descrpition. It has visiually appealing bolded headers and subheaders that direct the pages structure.

It makes sense to me, right when you get to the website you have a welcome page. After that you have advertising with reciepes, new products, information. They have navigation and shortcuts accesible at all times in the websites; it's just an appealing and functional website overall. They definitey are user-friendly and have an amazing UX team.

The graphics and layout enhance the site because they match the brand’s identity. The images are high quality and visually appealing, which makes the site more engaging instead of distracting. That being said, you can also interact with some of the graphics as they serve as shortcut links to a product or additional information. The placement and size of the images are convienent and attracts you to what it should!

The homepage loaded quickly, taking only a couple of seconds. I had no issues with the homepage either once it was fully loaded which is an important factor as well.

The text color and size was perfect, I also enjoyed the font they picked out. It's different from a regular font, it stands out and looks good, but it's still easy to read. They primarily use black as the text color, but it's a great choice since the background of the website is white, overall it makes the words more visible to the user. The website also has great sizing with headings and paragraph text. Overall everything worked together to enhance and attract to the site.

The navigation in this site is top notch. I definitely am a big fan of it. It incldues a side menu with shortcuts to different parts of their webpage. Also there is a hamburger menu with all the information you could need. If you need to find something quickly, they even have a search bar at the top of the page. Once you reach the bottom of the page, they have additional shortcuts to various differnet parts of their website. It's a 10/10 in the navigation area.

It's very consistent with the word font and color. But half the website feels more modern, while the other half feels more old-fashioned. They do a good job at blending in the scheme, but in the end it does stand out a bit. So overall, I wouldn't say it's repeated on all the pages.

I used a laptop and phone for this example. Every descrption I have been doing has been based off my experience with the website on laptop, but the website looks great on both devices. I actually enjoyed the phone version of the website more than the laptop. The website is defintiley flexible and versatile in the responsive design. It holds it's ease of navigation and other qualities!

This website does an amazing job at navigation. It's not just the computer version that has this quality access, but also phones. I would assume most people visiting this website would be on a mobile device, but the Trader Joe's website team did such an incredible job with navaigation features for whatever device. It stands out because the features are accesible throughout all the webpage, it doesn't matter if you're at the top, bottom, or middle of the webpage. I clicked on a a variety of links and shortcuts, they all seemed to work perfeclty without isses and a fast response time!
